Call for Participation

We invite researchers, designers, and practitioners interested in topics of vulnerability, embodiment, care, and ethics in HCI and design to participate in this workshop. The workshop brings together participants who are curious about how vulnerability is lived, sensed, and negotiated through bodies, relationships, and interactions, particularly in research and design practices engaging with health, wellbeing, care, life transitions, or other sensitive contexts.

We welcome participants from diverse methodological backgrounds, including (but not limited to) participatory design, soma design, embodied interaction, qualitative and interpretive research in sensitive contexts, artistic or practice-based research, and critical or feminist approaches. Prior experience with embodied methods is not required.

To apply, participants are invited to submit a 2-4 page position paper (including references, in the SIGCHI format). We also welcome pictorials provided they clearly communicate the authors’ context and perspective in relation to the workshop themes. Submissions can be made by individual authors or in collaboration. Submissions should describe:

  • The authors’ background and interests
  • A research or design context where vulnerability was encountered, negotiated, or felt (by researchers, participants, or both)
  • One or two questions, tensions, methodological and ethical considerations or moments of uncertainty the applicant would like to explore through the workshop

Submissions should also briefly note any particular needs or considerations the organizers should be aware of. At least one author of each accepted submission must attend the workshop in person. Accepted contributions will be shared with participants ahead of the workshop to support a reflective and attentive collective process.
